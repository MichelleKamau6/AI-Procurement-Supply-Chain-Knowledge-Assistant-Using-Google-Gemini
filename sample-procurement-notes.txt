PROCUREMENT & SUPPLY CHAIN MANAGEMENT NOTES

1. TENDERING
Tendering is the procurement process of inviting vendors to submit bids for goods or services. It ensures transparency, competition, and value for money. The tendering process includes:
- Preparation of tender documents
- Advertisement and invitation to bid
- Bid submission and opening
- Evaluation and award

2. CONTRACT MANAGEMENT
Contract management is the systematic process of managing contract creation, execution, and analysis to maximize operational and financial performance. Key roles include:
- Contract monitoring and compliance
- Performance tracking against KPIs
- Risk management and mitigation
- Dispute resolution
- Contract renewal or termination
- Documentation and record keeping

3. PROCUREMENT PROCESS
The procurement process consists of several stages:
a) Need identification
b) Supplier selection
c) Purchase order creation
d) Order fulfillment
e) Receipt and inspection
f) Invoice processing and payment
g) Record maintenance

4. TYPES OF CONTRACTS
- Fixed-price contracts: Set price regardless of costs
- Cost-reimbursement contracts: Buyer pays actual costs plus fee
- Time and materials contracts: Payment based on time and materials used
- Framework agreements: Long-term arrangements with pre-agreed terms

5. SUPPLY CHAIN MANAGEMENT
Supply chain management involves coordinating and managing all activities involved in sourcing, procurement, conversion, and logistics. It includes:
- Demand planning
- Inventory management
- Warehouse operations
- Transportation and distribution
- Supplier relationship management

6. PROCUREMENT ETHICS
Ethical procurement practices include:
- Transparency in all dealings
- Fair competition
- Conflict of interest avoidance
- Confidentiality of supplier information
- Compliance with laws and regulations
